Do you know Kakum National Park?

Kakum National Park is a home for nature lovers. It is located in a tropic jungle in the coastal environs of Ghana with a canopy walk. The canopy walkway consists of seven separate bridges that hang over 130 feet above the ground. The Park was established in 1931 as a reserve. There are many endangered species in this forest such as Diana Monkey and African Elephant. To get around, it is advisable to drive to the park. Walking is the most ideal option for getting around in the park. You can stay in Cape coast since there are many different tourist sites in that region.
